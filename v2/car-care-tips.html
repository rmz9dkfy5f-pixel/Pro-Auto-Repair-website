Car Care Tips

Practical, science‑backed ways to save fuel, extend part life, and stay safe — written in plain English.

← Back to Home

Maintenance That Pays Off

  • Follow the factory schedule in your owner’s manual for tune‑ups and inspections — it preserves performance and fuel economy.
  • Use the manufacturer‑recommended oil grade. Using the wrong viscosity can reduce mileage; the correct grade can improve fuel economy by about 1–2% if you were using the wrong one.
  • Fix dashboard warnings quickly. A glowing check‑engine or brake warning means stop guessing and get a scan/inspection.

Tires: Pressure, Rotation & Alignment

  • Check tire pressure monthly when “cold.” Use the door‑jamb placard (not the sidewall max). Proper inflation improves safety, tread life, and fuel use.
  • Rotate on schedule and align as needed. Uneven wear or a pulling wheel signals an alignment check and possible suspension inspection.
  • Mind your load and speed ratings. Overloading or sustained high‑speed driving generates heat and accelerates wear.

Choose the Right Fuel

  • Use the octane your manual calls for. Most vehicles are built for regular 87. Premium offers no benefit in engines designed for regular.
  • Seal the system. Make sure your gas cap is present and tight to prevent evaporation losses and emissions.

Smart Habits That Save Gas

  • Avoid extended idling. Modern engines don’t need long warm‑ups; shut down if you’ll be stopped for a while.
  • Smooth driving. Moderate acceleration, steady speeds, and looking ahead to avoid hard braking all pay dividends.
  • Cut weight and drag. Empty the trunk, remove unused roof racks, and close windows at highway speeds; use A/C reasonably.

Fluids & Visibility

  • Brake fluid, coolant, power steering, and washer fluid should be checked routinely; replace coolant and brake fluid on schedule.
  • Wiper blades & lights: Replace streaky blades and burnt bulbs promptly; you can’t fix what you can’t see.

Quick Checklist

  • Monthly: tire pressures (including spare), exterior lights, washer fluid.
  • Every oil change: inspect brakes, battery terminals/cables, belts, hoses, and filters.
  • Seasonal: test A/C performance, check coolant strength before winter, inspect wipers.
